VIHand: Enhancing 3D Hand Pose Estimation with Visual-Inertial Benchmark

Code Dataset Supplementary materials

VIHand is the first large-scale glove-worn dataset for visual-inertial hand pose estimation, containing over 1.4 million synchronized RGB-D and IMU frames from 15 subjects. It provides frame-level 3D joint annotations across complex hand gestures, enabling cross-modal semantic alignment and complementarity for advanced hand pose estimation research.

Abstract

Accurate and robust 3D hand pose estimation (HPE) plays a crucial role in human-computer interaction (HCI), augmented reality (AR) and virtual reality (VR) applications. However, visual-based approaches often suffer from limited fields of view, occlusions, motion blur and suboptimal lighting. Approaches based on inertial measurement units (IMUs) face limitations including susceptibility to magnetic interference, cumulative integration errors, and significant drift, which can severely degrade HPE accuracy. To address these challenges, we present VIHand, the first large-scale glove-worn dataset for visual-inertial HPE, comprising over 1.4 million synchronized RGB-D and IMU frames from 15 subjects. It includes frame-level 3D joint annotations spanning complex gestures, filling the gap in multimodal data and enabling cross-modal semantic alignment or complementarity. Based on VIHand, we propose a VIFNet framework, which integrates visual and inertial signals through an efficient cross-modal interaction strategy to achieve high-precision and robust pose estimation. To further explore the benefits of VIHand in practical IMU-only configurations, we futher propose VIFNet-S via cross-modal knowledge distillation, where rich joint representations from VIFNet are transferred to guide the student. This framework supports both multimodal fusion and practical deployment under IMU-only configurations, significantly enhancing the robustness and generalizability of 3D HPE. Extensive experiments demonstrate its advantages and potential in multimodal fusion, optical robustness, and IMU-only configurations. The dataset will be publicly released at https://github.com/Shirley0118/VIHand.

Method

Overall framework. The teacher model (VIFNet) takes a monocular RGB image and sparse IMU data as input, extracts spatial and temporal features, and fuses them via multi-head attention. The fused features are then decoded by the MANO head to estimate 3D hand pose. The student model (KDINet), trained on IMU data alone, learns to mimic the teacher's fused representation through knowledge distillation.
